For keeping your skin healthier, here are some tips might you know!

Wear sunscreen everyday

One of the most crucial things you can do for your skin is to wear sunscreen. When properly applied, sunscreen shields your skin from the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays, which are responsible for sunburn, skin cancer, and early signs of aging including wrinkles and dark circles.

Avoid using tanning beds.

Similar to the sun, tanning beds produce skin cancer-causing UV radiation.

Make your skin-care routine simpler.

When caring for your skin, less is more. Many anti-aging products, in particular, can irritate your skin if you use too many creams.

Invest on skin care items designed for your skin type.

You can choose the best skin care products for you by choosing those that are appropriate for your skin type and learning how to take care of it.

Care for your lips.

Use lip balm or lipstick containing sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher before going outside because skin cancer can develop on the lips. Use petroleum jelly to increase moisture if your lips feel chapped or dry.

Stop touching your face with your hands.

Your hands' oil, filth, and bacteria are transferred to your face each time you touch it. Try to keep your skin alone as much as possible during the day. Scarring can result from popping, plucking, or squeezing pimples.

Examine your skin frequently.

Regularly checking your skin for new spots, spots that differ from other places on your body, or moles that itch, bleed, or change color is crucial because these are frequently early indicators of skin cancer. Get a consultation with a board-certified dermatologist if you come across any questionable spots.

avoid cheap and fake skin care

Always check/verify your skin care if it authentic to avoid skin problems.
